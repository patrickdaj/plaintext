# Lab 16 — Write the Engagement Report

## Setup
Your findings and deliverables from the earlier Track 01 modules (recon, scan, vuln-id,
exploitation, web, privesc), plus a real report or two from the
[public collection](https://github.com/juliocesarfort/public-pentesting-reports) to model.

## Scenario
Turn the work from this track into a single professional penetration-test report a client could
act on.

> Report only on systems you were authorised to test.

## Do
1. [ ] Write an **executive summary**: the business-level "what we found and what it means," no
   jargon.
2. [ ] Write up each **finding**: title, affected asset, evidence, reproduction steps, impact,
   and a risk rating (CVSS + business context).
3. [ ] **Prioritise** the findings by real risk — not raw CVSS — and justify the order (use the
   KEV/EPSS thinking from module 03).
4. [ ] Write **remediation** for each finding that a defender could actually implement.

## Success criteria — you're done when
- [ ] Your report has an executive summary a non-technical reader understands.
- [ ] Every finding has evidence, reproduction, impact, and an actionable fix.
- [ ] Findings are prioritised by real-world risk, with the reasoning shown.
- [ ] It reads like the real public reports you studied.

## Deliverables
`engagement-report.md` (or PDF) — the full report. **This is the Track 01 capstone artifact**
and your strongest portfolio piece.

## AI acceleration
Have AI draft the prose and tighten the language from your raw notes — then verify every
finding, CVE, number, and reproduction step yourself. You own every claim in a report with your
name on it.

## Connects forward
This is the capstone of Track 01 — and the deliverable mindset (clear, evidence-backed,
actionable) carries into every other track's reporting.

## Marketable proof
> "I write professional pentest reports — executive summary, evidence-backed findings,
> risk-based prioritisation, actionable remediation — modeled on real industry reports."

## Automate & own it
**Required.** Build a reusable report template (and optionally a script that assembles findings
from your per-module notes into the report skeleton); AI drafts, you own the content; commit the
template.

## Stretch
- Stand up Ghostwriter and produce the same report through it, to see how teams manage findings
  at scale.
